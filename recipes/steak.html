Juicy Steak

Description

A simple but delicious steak. Seared and crispy on the outside, rare and juicy on the inside. Only requires butter, salt, pepper, and fresh garlic.

Ingredients

  • Fresh cut of steak with decent marbling
  • Salt
  • Black Pepper
  • Butter
  • Fresh Garlic

Steps

  1. Turn heat on high and allow cast iron to soak up heat as evenly as possible, occasionally shifting pan slightly
    2. While cast iron is heating:
  2. Grab steak, sprinkle coarse sea salt or himalayan rock salt, and freshly ground pepper to taste on one side
  3. Repeat on other side
  4. Once pan is heated, add butter and turn heat to low-med. Add coarsely crushed garlic
  5. Once air above butter is "wavering", before butter smokes, add steak to cast iron
  6. There should be a healthy sizzle, but not too agressive, and it will calm down as the steak absorbs heat :-)
  7. If garlic in danger of burning, scoop it on top of the steak to let its flavor soak down into the steak
  8. After 1-3 minutes, steak should develop a nice crust and separate from pan effortlessly. Flip steak to other side
  9. Cook this side 2-4 minutes, waiting for equivalent crust
  10. With tongs, flip steak on narrow side and sear each side for ~1 minute each
  11. Interior at this point is probably in rare to medium-rare territory
  12. If desired, cover pan and set to low till steak reaches desired internal temp
  13. Remove steak and let rest on a plate a few minutes before serving
